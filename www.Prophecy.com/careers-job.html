Prophecy Technologies is looking for a Power Platform Architect

A Power Solution Architect is responsible for the successful design, implementation, deployment, and adoption of an overall solution. A Solution Architect ensures that the technical solution meets the business use case needs now and in the future. While the Solution Architect may not “click the buttons” during a deployment, they are ultimately responsible for the solution and work closely with the business and their technology team. This role requires understanding how decisions affect the overall solution.
Candidates should have knowledge and experience of the Dynamics 365 customer apps ecosystem and Microsoft Power Platform. In addition, they have extensive domain knowledge in Finance industry verticals. The ideal candidate should be able to facilitate solution design decisions across development, configuration, integration, infrastructure, security, licensing, availability, storage, and change management. In this role, you balance project's business needs while meeting functional and non-functional requirements.

  • Architect solutions (20–25%)
  • Define technology solution strategies (35–40%).
  • Define Patterns (20–25%)
  • Remain Engaged in large projects (10–15%)
    • Job Responsibilities:
  • Liaise with business users across locations to understand business requirements as a technical resource to help field questions on possible Power platform based technical solutions.
  • Perform solution envisioning and requirement analyses considering the business use case needs and then developing those needs into a concept of a proposed technical solution.
  • A Solution architect assumes the role of reviewer to ensure that the designs fit within the desired architecture, and in alignment with best practices for Microsoft Power Platform and Dynamics 365.
  • Learn about the business current application architecture, and their desired outcomes and to independently generate ideas on how to approach a solution for that use case.
  • Have a deep awareness of the different pre-built patterns and available integrations that can be combined to highlight a proposed solution.
  • The solution architect might not be the one building the POC/demo, the role will be indispensable in helping to envision and craft what the POC or demo will highlight.
  • Ability to communicate with all levels of a customer's organization. The role effectively should translate technical topics into a more business focused discussion.
